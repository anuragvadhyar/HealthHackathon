Information about menopause

Changes in your hormones during menopause can impact your mental health as well as your physical health. You may experience feelings of anxiety, stress or even depression. Menopausal symptoms may include:

  • anger and irritability
  • anxiety
  • forgetfulness
  • loss of self-esteem
  • loss of confidence
  • low mood and feelings of sadness or depression
  • poor concentration – often described as 'brain fog' and/or lost words


Womenopause is a website to help you understand and recognise mental health difficulties while going through menopause. Especially those women from orthodox households can seek help on their own and start making the necessary steps towards recovery.

Diet Plan

Menopause diet plan to boost your energy and stabilize your mood

1. Pick the right grain

Rice, wheat, corn, millets- ragi, jowar, bajra, oats, quinoa, buckwheat, barley – all these and their products come under the grain category.


2. Consume legumes/ dal regularly

We often miss dal in our daily diet. There are days when you may have only roti or rice with vegetables skipping the dal.If you are a vegetarian, make a conscious effort to consume 1-2 bowls of dal/ legumes every day

3. You need good fat

Consider using kachhi ghani mustard oil or double-filtered groundnut oil/ sesame oil or cold-pressed coconut oil etc. The thumb rule is that the cooking oil should have its color and flavor.


Supplements that can be taken:

  • Black Cohosh: Help for Hot Flashes
  • Flaxseed: Easing Night Sweats
  • Vitamin D: Get Some Sun
  • Wild Yam: Alternative to Hormones
  • Ginseng: Mood Booster

Meditation

Meditation has been practiced for thousands of years. Meditation originally was meant to help deepen understanding of the sacred and mystical forces of life. These days, meditation is commonly used for relaxation and stress reduction. Meditation is considered a type of mind-body complementary medicine. Meditation can produce a deep state of relaxation and a tranquil mind. During meditation, you focus your attention and eliminate the stream of jumbled thoughts that may be crowding your mind and causing stress. This process may result in enhanced physical and emotional well-being.

Yoga poses

  • Keeping torso facing forward, bend right knee so thigh is parallel to floor and stretch arms out to sides.
  • Turn head to right and gaze over front fingertips. Hold 20 to 30 seconds or for 3 to 5 breaths.
  • Repeat on opposite side.
